📖 How to Play & Updates

⌨️ Full Controls Reference
W A S D — Move forward / left / back / right Space — Jump · hold vs wall to climb · swim up Shift — Sprint (drains fatigue) · dive underwater LMB hold — Mine / break block (hold to progress) RMB — Place held block in world Scroll / 1–2 — Switch hotbar slot E — Eat / drink held item Q — Drop held item on ground F — Forage · fish · butcher carcass · board/dismount raft X — Physical crafting · glue blocks when holding Pine Resin G — Blow air onto ember / fan flame / ignite kiln B — Apply bandage (need stick in inventory) R — Rotate held block · carve notch in wood Z — Sleep in placed bed to restore health & fatigue M — Open full world map (click to add waypoints) K — Knowledge panel (shows what you have learned) Tab — Stats panel (health, hunger, temp, fatigue) N — Toggle night vision F3 — Debug overlay (coords, biome, geology, FPS) Enter — Open chat Esc — Pause / options menu Alt — Free mouse cursor without unpausing
🧗 Wall Climbing — Full Explanation
Wall climbing lets you scale any solid vertical surface — cliff faces, canyon walls, built structures, even trees.
🟡 How to start climbing: walk directly into a solid block face so your body is touching it, then hold Space. You will begin moving upward along the surface.
🟡 Steering: use A / D to traverse sideways along the wall while climbing. You can move diagonally by pressing Space + A or Space + D simultaneously.
🟡 Speed: climbing speed is slower than running. Sprint (Shift) does not apply while climbing — only your base movement speed counts.
🟡 Fatigue cost: every second of climbing drains fatigue. If your fatigue reaches zero mid-climb, you stop climbing and may fall. Eat before long climbs.
🟡 Letting go: release Space to detach. You will then fall freely. Time your release to land on a ledge rather than plummeting.
🟡 Getting out of water onto cliffs: swim to the base of the cliff, face the rock face directly, and hold Space. This is often the only way out of deep ocean coastlines.
🟡 Works on: stone, dirt, wood, sand, planks, limestone, granite — any opaque solid block. Does NOT work on water, leaves, or air gaps.
⚠️ Watch out: overhang blocks will stop your ascent. Plan your route around them. If you get stuck under an overhang, traverse sideways to find a clear column.
🏁 Survival Priority — First Hour
1. 🍇 Eat immediately — forage berries (F near berry bushes) or grab fruit from fruit trees. Hunger drains fast.
2. 💧 Drink — walk into a river or lake and stand still to drink. Avoid ocean water — it dehydrates.
3. 🌲 Chop a tree — hold LMB on trunk blocks. You get Wood + Sticks fall randomly. Collect both.
4. 🔥 Make fire before dark — hold a Stick, look at a Wood or Root block on the ground, press X repeatedly until a carved notch forms. The friction meter fills — keep pressing. When it completes, an Ember drops. Pick it up, stand near dry Leaves, hold the Ember and press G to blow. The fire needs more wood placed on it to grow.
5. 🛏 Make a bed — place Leaf blocks on the ground and have a Stick in inventory, press X. Sleep (Z) to restore health overnight.
6. 🪨 Find flint — look near riverbeds, cliff faces, and coastal rocky shores for grey angular flint blocks. Essential for your first knife.
⛵ Raft Building
Place Boat Hull or Plank blocks on water → raft forms in ~1s → press F to board. See the ⛵ Rafts tab for the complete guide.
⛵ Raft Building — Complete Guide
⚡ Quick Start — 3 steps
1. Hold a Boat Hull or Plank block → aim at the water surface → RMB to place it.
2. Wait ~1 second for the raft scanner to register it.
3. Walk up to it and press F to board. You're sailing.
🪵 Block Types & Densities
⬜ Boat Hull (32) — density 0.52 🟢 floats well 🟫 Plank (26) — density 0.46 🟢 lightest hull option 🪵 Mast (33) — density 0.68 🟡 floats but heavy ⬜ Sail (34) — density 0.04 🟢 barely weighs anything 🌿 Leaves (6) — density 0.22 🟢 raises the raft 🟫 Dirt (2) — density 1.45 🔴 sinks slowly 🪨 Stone (3) — density 2.65 🔴 sinks fast Water = 1.0. Below 1.0 floats. Above 1.0 sinks.
Sinkage depth is recalculated every frame using Archimedes' principle — the raft rides exactly as deep as its mass demands. F3 overlay shows live depth, roll angle, and speed while riding.
🔨 Building Tips
📐 Shape matters — a wide flat raft (3×3 Planks) resists tipping far better than a tall narrow one. More columns of blocks = more stable base.
⚖️ Load balance — the raft tilts toward where your heavy blocks are. Put heavy blocks only on the left and it leans left. Spread mass symmetrically for a flat ride.
⬆️ Building upward — stack a second layer on top of your base hull. Those blocks are above the waterline so the scanner ignores them by default. Hold Pine Resin → press X nearby to glue them in so they count as part of the raft.
⛵ Add a sail — place a Mast block on the deck, then a Sail block on top of the mast. Both required. Wind assist scales with how directly downwind you're sailing — headwind gives nothing.
🔧 Edit while floating — you can mine blocks off the raft while standing on it or wading beside it. Physics update within ~1.5 seconds. Break heavy blocks off to stop a sinking raft.
⚠️ Sinking & Rescue
A raft sinks when total block mass > total block volume. You'll get a warning and the raft starts descending.
🛟 Rescue: break the heaviest blocks off (Stone first, then Dirt) with LMB. The moment mass drops below capacity the raft stops sinking and bobs back up.
🌊 If it hits the floor: it stays there but you can still break blocks off it — they go to your inventory. Rebuild from what's left.
Tide raises the water surface — a raft that barely floats at low tide can sink at high tide. Build with a margin.
🍯 Pine Resin & Glue
Pine Resin spawns as small glowing amber cubes near tree trunks. About 38% of trees have 1–2 nodes. Walk within arm's reach to auto-collect.
Resin is only needed for elevated blocks above the waterline — upper decks, masts, sails placed on top of other hull blocks. Hold resin in your hotbar, press X near those blocks, golden sparkles confirm the seal.
One resin seals all nearby hull blocks within ~2m. You do not need one per block.
Tip — look for the amber glow at trunk height near rivers and coastlines where trees grow close to water.
🕹️ Riding Controls
W / S — Forward / reverse A / D — Turn left / right Shift — Speed boost (×1.32) F — Dismount (spawns beside raft) LMB hold — Break blocks off raft while riding F3 — Live sinkage / roll° / speed readout
❤️ Health, Hunger & Status
❤️ Health (HP) — max 100. Restores naturally when hunger >70% AND thirst >70%. Sleeping restores HP faster. Falls, animals, bleeding, cold, and disease all drain it.
🍗 Hunger — eat food to keep it above 50%. Below 20% health drains at 3 HP/s. Raw meat gives hunger but risks disease. Cooked meat gives more and is safe.
💧 Thirst — stand in fresh water (river/lake) to drink passively. You can also drink rainwater outdoors. Boiled water (pot over fire) is the safest source. Ocean water dehydrates you faster than drinking nothing.
🌡️ Temperature — shown top-centre. Below 15°C your body takes slow damage. Wind-chill, wet clothing, and rain all lower it. Stay near campfires (+12°C), wear scraped clean hides, and build shelter to block wind.
💤 Fatigue — sprinting, climbing, and every X-key labor press drains it. At zero: climbing stops, sprint disabled, crafting slows. Sleep in a bed (Z) to fully recover.
🩸 Bleeding — animal bites and heavy falls cause bleeding. Press B with a Stick in inventory to bandage. If left untreated, bleeding transitions to wound infection after ~60s.
🤒 Infection → Sepsis — infected wounds drain HP rapidly. Eat well, sleep, and stay warm to let your immune system fight it. There is no medicine yet — prevention is the only cure.
🤧 Flu — prolonged wet + cold exposure triggers respiratory illness. Symptoms: slower movement, faster hunger drain. Shelter + fire + sleep are the treatment.
🌦️ Weather, Environment & Movement
🌧️ Rain — wets you (raises wetness %). This lowers temperature and makes fire-starting much harder. A built roof or cave overhang overhead blocks rain. You can drink rainwater by standing exposed.
🌪️ Tornadoes — yellow warning flashes in the HUD. Run perpendicular to the tornado's path immediately. Being caught inside deals near-fatal damage and launches you airborne. Tornadoes last 35–80 seconds and destroy placed blocks in their path.
🏔️ Seasons — four seasons affect food availability, temperature, and tree appearance. Winter freezes rivers, kills bushes, and makes nights lethal without shelter. Plan food storage before winter arrives.
🌊 Tides — the ocean rises and falls every few in-game hours. Coastal plains flood at high tide. Fish bite more readily during the incoming tide.
🤿 Swimming & Diving — walk into water to swim. Hold Shift to dive. Press F underwater to forage the seafloor: shells, clams, and occasionally pearls. Watch your oxygen bar — it depletes underwater and you surface automatically at zero.
🌊 Ocean currents — deep ocean has directional currents that push you. Fighting a current drains fatigue fast. Swim diagonally to escape rather than directly against it.
🍄 Glow Shrooms — appear in caves below y=−8, glowing faint cyan. Edible raw. Harvest with bare hands. The glow helps you navigate dark caves without fire.
🌅 Day/Night cycle — nights are pitch black without a light source. Wolves are more aggressive at night. Build a fire or use night-vision (N) to see.
🔥 Fire — Full Detail
Bow-drill method: hold Stick → look at a Wood or Root block → press X repeatedly. Each press fills the friction meter. Keep pressing until the meter reaches the target and an Ember spawns on the ground.
💧 Wetness penalty: rain and high body wetness actively drain the friction meter between presses. Dry conditions are far easier. The more you practice fire-making, the more your skill resists damp conditions.
🔥 Ember → flame: pick up the Ember (walk over it). Hold it in your hand. Stand next to dry Leaves or a prepared tinder bundle and press G to blow. The leaves will ignite. Place Wood blocks on or next to the fire to build a proper campfire.
🌿 Campfire structure: place 3 Wood blocks in a cluster, add Leaves on top, then ignite with an Ember. A campfire provides warmth (+12°C in range), light, and a cooking surface.
⚠️ Fire spread: fire can spread to nearby wood and leaf blocks in dry conditions. Don't build fires inside wooden structures.
⚙️ How Physical Crafting Works
There are no crafting menus. Every item is made through repeated physical actions — pressing X multiple times while holding the right material and being in the right situation. A progress bar appears on screen during active labor. If you stop pressing X for ~8 seconds, the progress resets. Fatigue drains with every press.
🪨 Stone Knife — Step by Step
1. Find flint — grey angular blocks near riverbeds, cliff bases, and coastal shores.
2. Rough the blank — hold Flint in hand, look at any stone boulder or rock block, press X ×4–6. Wrong angles shatter the flint (more likely early on). A rough blank forms.
3. Thin the edge — still looking at stone, X ×3–5. The blank becomes a sharpened edge (item changes name). A sharp edge can cut as a bare tool but has no handle.
4. Carve a handle groove — have a Stick in your other inventory slot, hold the edge, press X ×4–5. You carve a notch into the stick.
5. Lash the blade — have Rope also in inventory (see rope-making below), press X ×5–8. Each press is one wrap of cordage. A visual counter shows your progress. When complete, the knife assembles and the stick and rope are consumed.
✅ Result: Primitive Knife — active in your slot. ~30–50 uses depending on how well you've practised. Shown in top-left HUD.
🪢 Rope — Step by Step
1. Get vine fibre — break leaf blocks (F or LMB on leaves, ~60% drop vine). Or: hold 3 Leaf items, press X ×3 to strip them into raw fibre.
2. Dry the fibre — stand near a campfire with vine fibre in your inventory. After ~30s a notification tells you the fibres are dry. Skipping this step makes weak rope that snaps under stress.
3. Separate strands — hold a vine, press X ×3. You pull the fibre apart along the grain.
4. Twist into rope — have a second vine in your inventory, press X ×4–6. Each press gets a coaching cue ("Twist tighter… Reverse-twist, then forward…"). Two vines are consumed and one Rope is produced.
⚠️ Wet/undried vine rope still works but is fragile. The game will tell you the quality when it's made.
🏺 Clay Pottery — Step by Step
1. Collect Raw Clay — blue-grey blocks found on river banks and in waterlogged soil near water.
2. Choose your form with your gaze — hold clay in hand, look up for a Pot, look down for a Brick, look level for a Bowl, or look sharply sideways for a Figurine.
3. Coil rings — press X ×6. Each press adds a coil ring. You can change form by changing gaze direction in the first 2 presses.
4. Dry it — place it near a campfire or leave in sunlight. 30s to dry (faster near fire). A notification confirms drying.
5. Fire-harden — stay near a campfire for another 18s after drying. The piece fires and becomes functional, often producing bonus items (Pot yields extra meat; Brick yields extra bricks).
🏹 Bow & Arrows — Step by Step
1. Carve stave — hold a Wood block in hand, look at a standing tree trunk or log, press X ×6. Each press removes bark, shapes limbs, and narrows the handle.
2. Tiller (bend limbs evenly) — needs a Stick in inventory. Press X ×4. You flex the bow, shave the stiff side, and check draw symmetry. Stick consumed.
3. String the bow — needs Rope in inventory. Press X ×5 to loop nocks and brace the string under tension. Wood + Rope consumed. Bow is now active.
4. Make arrows — hold a Sharpened Flint Edge (type 43), have a Stick + Vine in inventory, press X ×3. Each arrow: split shaft → notch nock → bind tip. Consumes edge, stick, vine.
5. Shoot — with the bow active, hold X to draw (a % power indicator appears), release X to fire. Arrow power scales with draw time. Check arrow count in bottom-left HUD.
⛏ Charcoal Kiln — Step by Step
1. Pile logs — place at least 3 Wood blocks in a compact cluster on the ground.
2. Cover with dirt — place 6+ Dirt or Grass blocks over and around the log pile, completely burying it.
3. Seal the kiln — hold Dirt in hand, stand near the mound, press X. The game detects the wood-under-dirt structure and seals it as a kiln.
4. Ignite — hold an Ember in hand, stand near the base, press G. The kiln begins smouldering. You can walk away.
5. Wait ~90 seconds — a HUD counter shows burn time. When done, a notification appears.
6. Dig open — mine the dirt blocks. Each wood log in the kiln yields ~0.8 charcoal on average.
✅ Charcoal burns longer than wood in fire, and is an ingredient for primitive glue (resin + charcoal powder).
🫁 Hide Scraping & 🧂 Salt Preservation
Hide Scraping: butcher an animal (F on carcass with knife) to get raw Hides. Hold Hide in hand, look at a log or plank block (the work surface), have a Bone or Stone in your other slot, press X ×8. Feedback narrates each stroke. Clean hides give better warmth than raw ones.
Salt: mine stone blocks in dry cave walls at depth −6 or lower (biome must be dry — check F3). ~12% chance per stone broken to yield a salt crystal, shown as a HUD counter.
Salt Curing: hold raw Meat in hand, have salt available, press X ×4 to rub it in. The meat's spoil timer is extended by 10×. Essential for storing food through winter.
🐾 Tracking & Other Systems
Animal tracks: animals leave small clay-coloured footprint marks on soft ground (dirt, mud, grass near rivers, especially after rain). Press F looking at a footprint to read its age ("very fresh — minutes old") and, with enough hunting experience, its direction of travel.
Other X-key actions: Spear (Sharpened Edge + Stick + Rope in inventory → X to lash, throw with X at range) · Snare Trap (Vine + Rope in inventory near ground → X) · Fishing Net (3 Vines → X, use in water with F) · Bed (Leaves on ground + Stick → X) · Wood splitting (hold Wood block → X ×4 → 3 Planks)
🔪 Tool durability — Flint Knife: 30–50 uses (HUD top-left). Flint Spear: 30 throws. Fishing Net: 4 uses. Bow: degrades after many arrows. All shown in HUD when active.
🌍 Biomes
🌿 Grassland — abundant trees, berry bushes, fruit trees, and animals. The best biome to start in. Rivers run through it carrying clay deposits on their banks.
🏜️ Desert — sparse trees, no rivers. Extreme daytime heat drains thirst rapidly. Obsidian shards appear near ancient volcanic features here. Navigate carefully — water is rare.
❄️ Tundra / Snow — permanently cold. Animals are scarce. Requires scraped hides, shelter, and fire at all times. Rivers freeze in winter, blocking drinking access.
🏔️ Mountains — steep terrain. Rich in Stone, Coal Ore, Crystal, and at altitude, copper seams. Dangerous falls if you don't use wall-climbing carefully. Best place to find iron ore at depth.
🌊 Ocean / Coast — swimming and diving required. Pearl and clam drops. Tides affect coastlines significantly. Fish are more plentiful here.
🪨 Geology & Resources
The underground is layered: Dirt (surface) → Stone (y<−3) → Limestone (y<−8) → Granite (y<−22) → Bedrock (y≤−38). Deeper = harder to mine, better ores.
🪨 Flint — spawns in limestone/chalk zones, riverbanks, and coastal rock. Mining stone in these zones has a chance to drop flint fragments. F3 shows the geology layer you're in.
🔴 Iron ore — rust-red veins appear in stone/limestone at depth y<−14. Requires a clay furnace and charcoal to smelt (future update).
🟤 Copper — green-oxidised seams in mountain rock, typically at mid-altitude. Softer than iron, easier to work cold.
🧂 Salt — white crystalline crusts on cave walls in dry biomes at depth y=−6 to −18. Mine stone blocks there (~12% chance per block).
⚫ Obsidian — jet-black glass shards near old volcanic terrain (desert biome, high altitude). Naturally sharper than flint — rare lucky-find cutting tools.
🏺 Raw Clay — blue-grey blocks near river banks and waterlogged low ground. Appears on the surface in the right biome band.
💎 Coal Ore — dark streaks in stone, depth 6–18. Burns long in fire. Used in charcoal and glue-making.
Use F3 to see your current depth (Y coordinate), biome value, and geological layer — essential for knowing where to dig.
🐾 Animals
🐄 Cow — peaceful until struck. Drops Raw Meat + Hide on death. Runs fast when scared — approach downwind for a better chance with a spear throw.
🐷 Pig — forages near trees, moves in loose groups. Drops meat. Will flee toward other pigs when attacked, alerting the group.
🦌 Deer — fastest animal in the game. Nearly impossible to chase down on foot. Use a bow + arrow, spear throw from cover, or a snare trap near their water source.
🐺 Wolf — hunts in coordinated packs at night. Will follow you if you approach too close. One wolf is manageable; three is lethal without a weapon. Kill the pack leader (largest) to scatter the rest.
🐟 Fish — use Fishing Net (F in water) or set a Snare Trap near a river. Fish bite more at high tide. Underwater foraging (F while diving) also finds shellfish.
🐾 Tracking: rain + soft ground = more visible tracks. Follow footprints toward a water source to intercept animals at predictable locations.
🌐 Multiplayer
🌍 Host — click Host World on the main menu to generate a world and get a Room Code. You become admin automatically.
🔑 Join — enter the host's Room Code in the Join panel, or navigate to ?join=CODE directly. The world state syncs instantly on connect.
💬 Chat — press Enter. Type /help for commands. Admin commands start with /.
🗺️ Fully shared world — all players share terrain, campfires, ropes, animals, clothing, and map markers in real time. Block changes, fires lit, and ropes placed all sync immediately.
👗 Clothing — other players' outfits are visible (depends on host's visibility setting). Hosts can lock outfit editing per-world or per-player from the Admin Panel.
⚔️ PVP — damage dealt to other players is server-validated and synced. Kill feed shows in chat for the whole room.
👑 Admin — the host gets the Admin Panel (ESC → Admin Panel). Controls: kick, summon, force-walk, lock world, set weather/time, manage outfit rules.

🦌 Deer — fastest animal in the game. Nearly impossible to chase down on foot. Use a bow + arrow, spear throw from cover, or a snare trap near their water source.
🐺 Wolf — hunts in coordinated packs at night. Will follow you if you approach too close. One wolf is manageable; three is lethal without a weapon. Kill the pack leader (largest) to scatter the rest.
🐟 Fish — use Fishing Net (F in water) or set a Snare Trap near a river. Fish bite more at high tide. Underwater foraging (F while diving) also finds shellfish.
🐾 Tracking: rain + soft ground = more visible tracks. Follow footprints toward a water source to intercept animals at predictable locations.
